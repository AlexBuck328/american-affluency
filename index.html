Quality of Life

Income, Wealth and Poverty in America

Map authored by Alexander Buck

Income, wealth and poverty are all interrelated. Without income it is nearly impossible to build wealth. Living at or below the poverty level is determined by income. In the United States, we are blessed with many opportunities. We are free to live where we like and work in any career field we choose. An invariable result of a society as free as ours with so many avenues available for us to choose, is an inequality of outcomes. Meaning, one full-time worker may earn less income than another full-time worker. These variations can lead to massive inequalities.


This map is a representation of each states ranking based on income, wealth and poverty statistics. An index value ranging from 7 to -7 was given to each data point. The index values represent deviation from national medians. The positive index values represent a positive deviation from the national median, while the negative index values represent a negative deviation from median. For example, median household income for the United States is $57,652. A zero index value represents a median income between $56,151 and $59,150. An index value of 7 represents the highest range of median incomes above the national median, while a -7 index value represents the lowest range median incomes below than national median. This process was applied to wealth and poverty datasets as well. After index values were assigned to income, wealth, and poverty, the values were summed to provide an overall index value. This method provides an over all quality of life measure relative to national median figures for each state.
